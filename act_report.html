It looks like our inference was correct. The rating_numerator is skewed to the left while favourite_count and retweet_count are skewed to the right.

Conclusion: Most dogs are likely to get a higher rating but more dogs are likely to get low retweet count and favorite count. This conclusion can be attributed to who controls the these values and how it affects them.

The user of WeRateDogs twitter handle doesn't lose much from giving higher ratings, actually it makes the rating system more unique. While followers control the retweet count. Retweeting or favorating a post takes valuable time out of their life, just like a company doesn't give high salary to most employees, followers dont give high retweet and favorite counts to most dogs.

Insight #3: Adjusted ratings¶

This makes me wonder, is this the reason that our rating_numerator distribution was negatively (left) skewed? What about a dog with a rating 9 in November 2015? Is that dog a 12 if I adjust for the dog rating inflation? How about we test this with our data.

Maybe we should have a subject in college called Doggonomics, I surely would take that class even if it is just trying to teach us the basics behind normalizing and inflation.

In order to do this analysis we need to divide the ratings by the mean rating of that month and then multiple it by the current average.

Result Summary¶

From the above analysis we saw the following:

  1. Descriptive stats
    • We see that the graph of rating numerator is left skewed while favorite count and retweet count are right skewed
  2. Average rating over time
    • Average ratings have changed increased over time indicating transition to a less strict rating system
  3. Adjusted rating
    • Used the average rating over time to normalize the ratings and adjust them to recent rating strictness
  4. Correlations
    • When grouped by time, increase in mean ratings is strogly correlated with increase in mean popularity
  5. Popularity by breeds
    • Visualized the popularity, ie the favorite count and retweet count of 15 highest rated breeds. Afghan Hound, Bouvier Des Flandres and Saluki were amongst the most popular breeds
